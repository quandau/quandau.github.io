Quan DAU is an engineer, hydrologist, and researcher in water resources management. He obtained his Master's degree in Civil Engineering from MSU-Iligan Institute of Technology, Philippines (2013), and a Ph.D. degree in Water Resources Engineering from Khon Kaen University, Thailand (2017). His research mainly focused on understanding climate and socio-economic changes in the water resources system. Particularly, he worked on the development of robust tools for sustainable planning and management of reservoirs, as well as hydrological simulations. He used machine learning, artificial intelligence paradigms, fuzzy inference, and data mining for analyzing complex environmental systems and predictions. Quan DAU is also a computer programmer. Latterly, he has developed various research tools and web-interface applications, which are now recognized by local stakeholders to aid their day-to-day operational management. Quan DAU has many years of experience working on various projects related to construction sites and water-related problems in Asia and Europe. He was an engineer at Gerona Enterprises Company in the Philippines (2012), and a research assistant at Water Resources Environmental Institute in Thailand (2015). He has consultative experience at UNESCO-Bangkok for a project Climate Change Vulnerability for Greater Mekong Sub-region (2015). He was then appointed as a research associate at Heriot-Watt University in the United Kingdom (2018) and the National Research Institute of Agriculture, Food and the Environment in France (2020). He also attracted external funding for his research as a co-Principal Investigator for Linking climate change, land-use change, and flood events in the lower Nam Phong River Basin: Impacts and damages, funded by Thailand Research Fund (TRF) during the period from 2018 to 2020. Scientifically, his research findings have been published internationally in both SCOPUS and Web of Sciences. He also joined various professional training conducted by the Karlsruhe Institute of Technology, UNESCO-IHE Institute for Water Education, and Danish Hydraulic Institute. Currently, he is an active member of the European Geosciences Union and International Association of Hydrological Sciences and serving as a consultant for UNESCO in Hanoi Vietnam.

EXPERTISE

My research area focuses on understanding water science and global climate change including, but not limited to, hydrological cycle, water resources planning and management, qualitative and quantitative water resources, remote sensing and spatial analysis, artificial neural network, climate change adaptation strategy, irrigation water management, socio-economic projection, reservoir optimisation, hydrological modelling, and natural disasters.
